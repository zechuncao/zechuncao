Overview

The popularity ranking of baby names from years 2001 to 2010 is downloaded from http://liveexample.pearsoncmg.com/data/babynamesranking2001.txt and stored in files named babynameranking2001.txt, babynameranking2002.txt, . . . , babynameranking2010.txt. Each file contains one thousand lines. Each line contains a ranking, a boy’s name, number for the boy’s name, a girl’s name, and number for the girl’s name. For example, the first two lines in the file babynameranking2010.txt are as follows: 

   1    Jacob   21,875      Isabella    22,731
   2    Ethan   17,866      Sophia      20,477

So, the boy’s name Jacob and girl’s name Isabella are ranked #1 and the boy’s name Ethan and girl’s name Sophia are ranked #2. 21,875 boys are named Jacob and 22,731 girls are named Isabella. Write a program that reads the files from URLs, and prompts the user to enter the year, gender, and followed by a name, and displays the ranking of the name for the year. Here is a sample run: 

    Enter the year: 2010
    Enter the gender: M
    Enter the name: Javier
    Javier is ranked #190 in year 2010
